The demand of IT jobs has seen a significant increase over the years with Australia’s technology workface seeing an average trend growth of 2.5% per annum between 2011 and 2018 which is outpacing growth in the overall Australian labor market (ACS Deloitte Australia’s digital pulse 2019). With this Australia’s technology workforce is forecast to grow by 100,000 workers between 2018 and 2024 with the health care industry seeing the fastest technology workforce growth, at 7.3% per annum (ACS, 2019). In addition to this the ICT services exports went from $2.93b in 2016-17 to $3.78b in 2017-2018 which has led to the ICT services trade services increase from $170m to $515m. (ACS, 2019).

The past and predicted data all point to an increase in demand for IT based jobs and therefore as all the ideal jobs proposed by our group members are IT related, it is likely for them to see an increase in demand by the time we complete this information technology course. Therefore, this would reinforce their opinions on their ideal jobs as their future seems more certain.

Job Opinions

Adrian:

Having looked at the data my opinion of what I deem to be my ideal job has not changed. Looking at the data regarding the ranking of jobs in terms of number of listings I can determine that the demand for my job is increasing by the year with being rank 22 in May 2017 to rank 8 by March 2018. This has given me greater confidence in the availability of similar jobs by the time I complete my Bachelor of Information Technology degree. In terms of skills, it’s only natural in a job about system administration, engineering and general IT support for there to be a requirement for communication and this also happens to the skill with the highest demand being rank 1 consistently. Second to this dealing with any issues that may arise from the system or individuals, problem solving are also very important skill which is supported by it being ranked 2 consistently in the burning glass data. As these skills are a given seeing them being placed at such as high rank on the burning glass data has not affected my judgement on the ideal job in any way.

Heath:

Having looked at the data, my opinion of my ideal job has not changed all too much, some may be surprised that there is as much demand for customer service and relationship building in a job about software development as there is, however one must remember that they are creating the software for a client, someone that is paying you to make something that only they know about, so being able to talk and communicate with them is essential to creating software that they find satisfactory. As I have known this for a while now, it did not change my opinion of my ideal job, everything else the burning glass data gave was already fairly widely known.
